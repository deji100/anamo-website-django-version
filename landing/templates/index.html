What is CDM?

The Continuous Diagnostics and Mitigation (CDM) Program offers a dynamic approach to enhancing the cybersecurity capabilities of businesses and government agencies, as well as the networks and systems they manage and protect. Created by the U.S. Department of Homeland Security, CDM is a vital security component under the Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency (CISA) in the United States.

The DHS and CISA developed the multi-billion-dollar CDM program to gather essential cybersecurity risk information and effectively present various categories on designated dashboards in near real-time.

The constant need in cybersecurity is deep forensic data collection. Unfortunately, many cybersecurity tools fail to effectively identify, collect, or interpret critical forensic data. For instance, systems with enabled logging often overlook critical alerts, treating them as white noise. Anamo addresses this by offering multi-level customization and prioritizing essential forensic information. This empowers InfoSec teams to precisely trace events, transactions, dates, times, and devices, improving their ability to respond to incidents effectively.

Anamo excels in essential forensic data collection, particularly in tracking and analyzing the attributes of users and group permissions. This capability ensures precise monitoring of access controls and helps in identifying security risks tied to permission misuse or misconfiguration.

Anamo seamlessly gathers user and group permissions from both Windows and Linux operating systems, displaying them in their native formats (numeric or symbolic). This automated process enables InfoSec administrators to interrogate key details such as hosts and paths, providing comprehensive oversight of permissions across diverse environments.

Anamo tracks even the most nuanced permission changes, allowing administrators to set the urgency of alerts for such events. It autonomously collects data, providing detailed forensic tracking and displaying both alerts and data in a logical format for efficient remediation and investigation. This ensures admins can respond swiftly to changes while maintaining deep visibility into their systems.

Anamo Software Forensics empowers InfoSec teams to quickly identify protected systems based on various criteria, including operating system, name, type, version (old and new), creation date, action, and other historical data. It enables filtering across selected timeframes and multiple criteria, simplifying the analysis of changes and actions taken within the system for improved cybersecurity management.

There are many types of “external scanners”, but they are not behind the firewall and limited to the type of data that the scanner can collect, this limitation leaves the customer with (potentially) knowing the problem but without the essential ability to receive remediation validation in real-time. Before one can anticipate achieving ZTA with any platform or service, entities will first require the initial ability to gather and understand correlated data. These trust indicators should include, at a minimum, Cybersecurity risk data related to identity attributes concerning Users, Groups, Port, and Permissions, all in an effort to ensure that they have correct and robust data forensics gathering capability for validation and verification purposes.

Anamo is essentially your trusted 3rd party validation system to any ZTA effort and provides the essential data sets needed to operationally validate a working ZTA system.

If you represent a DoD supply chain operator or have a federal regulatory compliance requirement such as CMMC, NIST 800, HIPAA, or SOX, you’ll feel right at home and well-accommodated by enhancing the ZTA program with Anamo CDM.

Simply stated, ZTA is a multi-faceted aspect of Cybersecurity which includes a clear understanding and the ability to track each and every User or Group of Users IDs within select computing systems. At a highly granular-level, Anamo tracks, documents, and even alerts InfoSec team members of any modifications of individuals, groups, ports, or permissions including Adds, Changes, Deletes, and more.

The GRC APP enables On-Demand assessment management, policy, procedure, and compliance management, as well as delivering instant Security System Plans (SSP) or Facility Security Plans (FSP). Additionally, the GRC manages compliance deadlines, POAM’s (Plan of Action & Milestones) and more.
